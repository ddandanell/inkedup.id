## B. Business-model clarity gap

**The contradictory defining sentences (all quoted from evidence, `_raw/04` finding 7):**

| Source | Quote |
|---|---|
| Footer (`Footer.tsx:61-64`) | "Bali's Premium Mobile Tattoo Concierge" / "Verified studios. Sterile mobile setup. We bring the studio to your villa." |

| FAQ (`FAQ.tsx:68`) | "It's our platform fee — the remaining 90% goes directly to **your artist**." |
| How It Works (`HowItWorks.tsx:111`) | "The remaining 90% is paid directly to **your studio** after the session is completed." |
| Terms (`Terms.tsx:40`) | "InkedUp partners with verified studios only, never individual freelance artists." |
| /studio/apply (`StudioApply.tsx:283`) | "Bring your studio, we bring the bookings." |

**Verdict from evidence (`_raw/04` finding 7):** the actual model is a *studio-partner marketplace/concierge that collects a 10% platform deposit and passes 90% to the studio* — but the site markets and books **individual artists** (artist profiles, per-artist "Book a Session"), the FAQ says 90% goes to "your artist" while How-It-Works says "your studio", and **no legal entity is named anywhere**, so the contracting party is ambiguous.

**The ONE positioning statement that must be owner-approved** (draft for sign-off — not for publication as-is; derived from `_raw/04` finding 7 fix):

> "InkedUp is Bali's mobile tattoo concierge. We partner exclusively with vetted tattoo studios — never freelance artists. You book through InkedUp; a verified artist from a partner studio performs your session at your villa or hotel with a fully sterile mobile setup. InkedUp collects a 10% platform deposit at booking; the remaining 90% is paid directly to the partner studio after your session."

Owner must confirm before use: (1) who the customer contracts with; (2) whether 90% goes to the *studio* (per Terms/HowItWorks) or the *artist* (per FAQ) — pick one and align all pages; (3) the registered legal entity name, registration number and address (currently TODO, `business.ts:9` — `_raw/04` OWNER-VERIFY 9; `_raw/08` OWNER-VERIFY 1); (4) who legally collects the 10% deposit and its refund terms (`_raw/04` OWNER-VERIFY 13). Until this is approved, every "concierge vs marketplace" and "artist vs studio" sentence on the site remains a latent contradiction (`_raw/04` finding 7, P1).

## E. E-E-A-T gap summary vs Google's people-first guidance

Google's people-first/E-E-A-T framing rewards content created *for* people by identifiable, experienced sources with verifiable claims — and the market evidence confirms it: the two best-ranking guides in this niche are first-person studio-authored pieces with named artists, concrete prices, and checkable specifics (Hustle Ink ~2,900 words; Social Ink House ~1,800 words with "7 years, 2,000+ clients", named ink brands, named hospitals), while thin generic copy ranks below them (`_raw/06` finding 4). A medical clinic ranks for tattoo hygiene queries — credentialed voices win (`_raw/07` finding 7). Against that standard:

| Dimension | Gap | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Experience** | No first-hand evidence anywhere: no real client work (shared stock portfolios), no real reviews (seed-only), invented testimonial personas, fabricated session counters. The honest live counters render "0+ Sessions Completed" — the site simultaneously fabricates volume and confesses zero track record | §A rows 2–7; `_raw/04` findings 2–5, 10 |
| **Expertise** | No named humans: no founders, no team, no authors, no medical/artist reviewers; YMYL-adjacent hygiene claims unattributed and unevidenced; near-duplicate artist profiles with fake availability | `_raw/04` findings 8, 12, 13 |

| **Trustworthiness** | The compounding failures: P0 fake contact number in every CTA + structured data; fabricated metrics (reviews, sessions, bookings); contradictory commercial info (prices/fees, 24/7 vs 8:00–20:00, artist-vs-studio payee); ambiguous contracting party; thin legal pages | §A, §B; `_raw/04` findings 1, 6–9, 11 |
