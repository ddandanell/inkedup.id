## Key Insights for InkedUp

1. **Large, growing, concentrated market**: 40,000-55,000 digital nomads with strong geographic clustering in Canggu, Pererenan, Ubud, and Uluwatu — ideal for mobile service delivery
2. **High-income, experience-seeking segment**: Average income $55K-124K/year with strong propensity to spend on experiences, convenience, and quality
3. **Tattoo-positive culture**: Tattooing in Bali is seen as a meaningful travel ritual, not just decoration — aligns with nomad values of self-expression and transformation
4. **Community-driven discovery**: Nomads rely heavily on peer recommendations in closed groups — excellent word-of-mouth potential
5. **Extended stays enable larger pieces**: 6-month visa stays and 5.7-week average per-location stays allow for multi-session work and proper healing
6. **Price-quality arbitrage**: Western-earning nomads perceive Bali tattoos as exceptional value — willing to pay premium prices by Bali standards while still feeling they got a deal
7. **Young, tech-savvy, social media active**: 75% Gen Z/Millennial, 89% use AI, high Instagram/TikTok engagement — mobile app booking and social proof essential
8. **Growth trajectory intact**: Despite global deceleration (2.2% YoY), employee-nomads grew 10% in 2025; 26% of young Americans plan to become nomads
